The AI Frontier Audit

Find the edge where AI stops helping you and starts quietly hurting you.

AI is flattening the easy parts of every job. The skill that now separates people is knowing which of your tasks to hand over, which to watch closely, and which to keep in your own hands. This audit maps your actual work onto that edge.

A free tool from the "Surviving Corporate America in the Age of AI" series · Takes about 5 minutes

How it works

List the recurring tasks in your role, the things you actually do in a normal week. For each one, set two dials. The combination places the task on the frontier and tells you how to treat it.

Dial 1 · Fit

How well-defined and pattern-based is this task? Work with clear inputs and a known shape of answer sits high. Work that depends on messy context, taste, or relationships sits low.

Dial 2 · Cost

If the output were subtly wrong and you did not catch it, how expensive would that be? A throwaway internal note is low. A board number, a client commitment, a legal line is high.

Why two dials instead of one. Most AI advice sorts tasks by whether the tool can do them. That misses the real risk. The 2023 Harvard and BCG study on AI and knowledge work found that people using AI on tasks outside its reach did meaningfully worse than people who used no AI at all, because the output looked right and quietly wasn't. Fit alone is a trap. Fit measured against the cost of being wrong is judgment. That second dial is the whole point.
